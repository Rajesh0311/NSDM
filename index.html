Independent research programme

AI should not only answer. It should know when it is justified.

NSDM studies the boundary between an answer an AI can produce and an answer it has enough evidence, authority, and context to produce.

In plain English: can the system tell the difference between sounding convincing and being safe enough to trust?

What NSDM actually does

Most AI evaluation asks whether a model is accurate, fluent, or useful. NSDM asks what must be true before its answer should influence a person, product, customer, company, or regulated decision.

Boundary 01

Evidence-state

Does the evidence support the claim, contradict it, fail to support it, or leave a necessary premise missing?

Boundary 02

Governance-state

Is the action allowed, forbidden, ambiguous, outside authority, audit-sensitive, or requiring human review?

Boundary 03

Action-state

Should the system answer, ask for clarification, request more evidence, abstain, refuse, escalate, or block?

Neurosymbolic AI

Pattern recognition meets explicit reasoning.

Neural AI recognises patterns in language, images, behaviour, and data. Symbolic AI represents rules, definitions, permissions, and logical relationships.

NSDM extends the hybrid into a decision layer: is the evidence sufficient, is authority clear, what is the consequence of being wrong, and what should the system do next?

Neural perceptionInterpret uncertain language, images, telemetry, or behaviour.
Symbolic representationMap facts into explicit concepts, rules, and relationships.
Constraint checkingTest the proposal against policy, logic, permissions, and known structure.
NSDM boundary evaluationLabel the evidence-state, governance-state, and permitted action.
Controlled responseAnswer, ask, verify, abstain, refuse, or escalate.
Working mathematical frame

Capability is not the same as admissibility.

Justified(A) is true only when E + R + C + K are sufficient for the action.
The greater the consequence of error, the stronger the evidence, control, and escalation requirements.
