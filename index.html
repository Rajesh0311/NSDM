NSDM is a research and product programme about a simple question: when an AI system gives an answer, should we trust it?

Not because it sounds confident. Not because it is fast. Not because it found something useful-looking. But because the evidence, rules, context and consequences actually justify the answer.

What NSDM means

The boundary between capability and justification.

NSDM stands for Neuro-Symbolic Decision Boundaries. The name sounds heavy, but the idea is simple.

A machine can produce an answer. That does not mean it should. NSDM studies the point where an AI answer has enough evidence, context and governance clearance to be acted on.

AI capability asks: Can the system produce an answer?
NSDM asks: Is the system justified in producing or acting on that answer?

The goal is to help people know when AI should answer, when it should ask for more evidence, when it should escalate to a human, and when it should stop.

The six labels

Before we trust the answer, we label the evidence.

Most AI systems collapse everything into a confident response. NSDM slows the moment down and asks: what state is the evidence actually in?

Supported

The evidence is strong enough for the claim being made.

Unsupported

The claim may sound useful, but the evidence is not there.

Contradicted

The available evidence points against the claim.

Under-specified

Something important is missing: source, context, method, permission, or proof.

Governance-ambiguous

The answer may be technically possible, but authority, policy or risk is unclear.

Reward-aligned but unjustified

The answer serves a target or incentive, but overstates what is actually known.

Examples

Where this matters in the real world.

NSDM is not abstract philosophy. It is for the messy places where AI decisions touch money, customers, trust, safety, governance and products that people actually use.

Customer decisions

If an AI says a customer should be blocked, downgraded, upsold or compensated, what evidence supports that action? Is a human review required?

QSR and retail choices

If a menu, promotion or checkout path changes behaviour, is it helping customers decide clearly or creating friction, confusion and manipulation risk?

Product verification

If AI wrote the code, did the code merely run, or did the product actually work? Can a user complete the intended flow?

Mathematical and logical claims

If an AI generates a candidate solution, has an independent verifier checked it? And is the story of how it was produced still under-specified?

Telecom AI governance

If a telco AI system recommends a network action, customer intervention or billing decision, who controls the evidence and who carries the risk?

Review and reputation signals

A review is not just positive or negative. It can signal trust, switching intent, price fairness, service recovery or warning behaviour.

Who this is for

Different stakeholders. Same problem: justified action.

Researchers

Use NSDM as a framework for evidence-state classification, benchmark design, verification experiments and AI decision-boundary research.

Enterprises and CIOs

Use it to decide when AI outputs are safe enough to use, when they need more evidence, and when a human must remain in the loop.

Governance teams

Use it to separate unsupported, contradicted, under-specified and governance-ambiguous claims before they become operational risk.

Product teams

Use it to verify whether AI-built products actually reach the intended user state, not just whether code executed.

Marketing and consumer teams

Use it to analyse decision friction, trust cues, switching signals, service recovery and behavioural evidence without pretending to read minds.

Investors and partners

Use it to understand why the next serious AI layer is not another chatbot, but evidence, provenance, governance and verification.
