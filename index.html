Boundary Thesis

Capability is not justification.

Modern AI systems can produce fluent, useful, and reward-aligned outputs that are still unsupported, contradicted, under-specified, or unsafe to act upon. NSDM makes this boundary explicit.

Fluency is not evidence.A grammatically correct, coherent response can still lack an adequate evidential basis for the claim it makes.
Reward is not justification.An output satisfying a reward signal or target metric may still violate a higher-order constraint or lack justification.
Missing premise is its own state.An inference that breaks at an unspecified premise is under-specified — it is neither unsupported nor contradicted.

Why this matters

Decision systems fail most dangerously when they collapse qualitatively different evidential states into one apparently confident output. NSDM is designed to preserve those distinctions, so that governance, evaluation, and action can remain accountable to the actual state of support.

Label Taxonomy

Six epistemic states. No collapsing allowed.

Each label captures a distinct relationship between evidence, rules, claims, and action. Collapsing them creates false precision and conceals the actual failure mode.

Evidence supplies a complete justification chain for the claim. All required premises are present and the inference is valid.
supported
Support relation holds.
supported when the available evidence, taken together with any applicable rules and context, is sufficient to construct a valid inference to the claim. No premise is missing. No inferential step is broken. The decision is epistemically grounded and the justification chain can be explicitly reconstructed.
No justification chain connects the available evidence to the claim. The claim is neither confirmed nor denied — the evidence is simply absent.
unsupported
Not negation.
unsupported when no rule, evidence, or justification applies. The landscape of evidence is empty with respect to the claim. No inference chain can be constructed from what is available — not because a premise is missing from an existing structure, but because no adequate structure exists at all.
The evidence actively supports the negation of the claim. A positive inference to ¬Claim can be constructed from the available premises.
contradicted
Contradiction holds.
contradicted when the available evidence does not merely fail to support the claim — it actively supports its negation. An inference to ¬Claim can be explicitly constructed from the premises in the same way a supported claim would be constructed.
A partial justification chain exists. The inference structure is identifiable but breaks at a specific missing or underspecified premise.
under_specified
Premise missing.
under_specified when a justification structure is identifiable and partially constructed, but a necessary premise is absent, ambiguous, or inadequately specified. Providing the missing premise could change the label to supported.
The applicable rule, authority, policy, or approval boundary is unclear, conflicting, discretionary, or hidden. The model cannot establish what governance regime controls the decision.
ambiguous_governance
Governance unclear.
ambiguous_governance when the rules, policies, authority structures, or approval boundaries that should govern the decision are themselves unclear, conflicting, discretionary, or hidden from the decision system.
The output achieves a target, optimises a metric, or satisfies a reward signal, but lacks adequate justification or violates a higher-order constraint.
reward_aligned_unjustified
Justification fails.
reward_aligned_unjustified when the system output satisfies a reward signal, optimisation target, or performance metric while simultaneously lacking adequate justification or while violating a higher-order constraint.
unsupported≠under_specified
Critical Distinction

unsupported

No adequate support — no rule, evidence, or justification applies. The landscape of evidence is empty with respect to the claim. You cannot construct a justification from what is available.

under_specified

The structure of support may exist. A justification could be constructed — but a necessary premise is absent. Provide the missing premise and the label may change to supported.

Formal Decision Object

Evidence, rules, claim, action.

Every benchmark item is grounded in a formal decision object. The structure makes implicit assumptions explicit and forces evidence states to be declared.

DecisionObject · reference schema
from dataclasses import dataclass
from typing import Any, List, Optional

@dataclass
class DecisionObject:
    """
    The atomic unit of NSDM analysis.
    All benchmark items are grounded in this structure.
    """
    x: Any                 # Input — prompt, query, or situational context
    r: List[str]           # Rules — governance rules or normative constraints
    h: Optional[List[Any]] # History — prior decision states or context memory
    y: str                 # Claim — proposition being evaluated or asserted
    a: str                 # Action — system output or recommended decision
    e: str                 # Ev. State — one of the six NSDM label classes
    c: float               # Calibration — confidence score in [0.0, 1.0]
Field semantics
FieldNameRole in the decision object
xInputThe raw prompt, query, or situational context presented to the decision system.
rRulesThe set of governance rules, policies, or normative constraints applicable to this decision.
hHistoryOptional list of prior decision states, conversation turns, or context that informs the current evaluation.
yClaimThe proposition or assertion whose justificatory status is being evaluated by the NSDM label.
aActionThe system's output, recommendation, or decision — the thing being justified, or failing to be justified.
eEvidence StateThe assigned NSDM label: one of supported · unsupported · contradicted · under_specified · ambiguous_governance · reward_aligned_unjustified.
cCalibrationA continuous confidence score in [0.0, 1.0] quantifying annotator certainty in the assigned evidence state.

A later branch: a safe superior intelligence cannot merely be obedient. It must be benevolent under uncertainty, regret-sensitive under action, and autonomy-preserving under asymmetric power.

Who this benchmark is for

NSDM is designed for researchers, model evaluators, governance teams, and enterprise decision-system builders who need to distinguish fluent output from justified output. It supports benchmark construction, model comparison, annotation protocol design, and evidence-state evaluation under explicit governance constraints.

  • Researchers can use the corpus to study evidence-state classification and epistemic failure modes.
  • Model evaluators can compare baseline systems under leakage-audited conditions.
  • Governance and assurance teams can use the taxonomy to separate unsupported, contradicted, and under-specified outputs.

Access pathway

The benchmark programme is progressing from a validated seed corpus toward open release. The current sequence is 60 examples, then inter-annotator agreement testing, then Paper 1 readiness, and finally scaled public benchmark release.

Research Governance

Principles for accountable decision science.

NSDM operates under a formal research governance charter. The following principles are binding on all benchmark design, annotation, analysis, and publication activities.

Source Verifiability

Every citation must resolve to a real, retrievable source. References that cannot be located or independently verified are treated as fabrication and are excluded from the NSDM record.

Data Integrity

Benchmark items, labels, agreement scores, and baseline results must arise from actual annotation and evaluation runs. Synthetic, estimated, or back-filled data are prohibited in both internal analyses and public outputs.

Falsifiability and Testability

Every research claim must be stated in a form that admits refutation. NSDM does not publish claims that cannot be operationalised into tests or refuting procedures.

Systematic Leakage Auditing

High accuracy before a leakage audit is not a result. Any benchmark item whose label or reasoning chain appears in a model's pre-training corpus is quarantined from evaluation and disclosure.

Architecture Follows Evidence

The cheapest possible experiment to validate the expensive assumption. New model components, label extensions, and benchmark dimensions are introduced only after the existing structure has been empirically validated.

Explicit Separation of Speculation

Quantum, holographic, Vedic, and other grand-unified material is quarantined from the academic core. Analogies are not proofs, and speculative ideas are maintained separately from validated findings.
